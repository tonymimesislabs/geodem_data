# Liverpool LGA Occupation Analysis: Labourers

## LGA Classification
Liverpool is a **suburban LGA** in southwestern Sydney, NSW. It's part of the Greater Sydney metropolitan area with significant residential development, industrial zones, and manufacturing facilities.

## Overall Assessment
The "Labourers" category appears to be **roughly typical** for Liverpool LGA, with the total workforce of 90,176 supporting a diverse mix of labourer occupations that align with a suburban industrial area.

## Most Over-Represented Occupations

### 1. **Baking Factory Worker** (2.0x expected rate)
- 51 projected workers
- Indicates presence of commercial bakeries and food manufacturing facilities

### 2. **Product Assembler** (1.5x expected rate) 
- 274 projected workers (largest over-represented group)
- Suggests significant manufacturing and assembly operations

### 3. **Food and Drink Factory Workers nec** (1.5x expected rate)
- 126 projected workers
- Points to a strong food processing and beverage manufacturing sector

### 4. **Chemical Plant Worker** (1.5x expected rate)
- 27 projected workers
- Indicates industrial chemical processing facilities in the area

## Most Under-Represented Occupations

### 1. **Beef Cattle Farm Worker** (far below expected)
- Expected in a suburban/urban environment with limited agricultural land

### 2. **Dairy Cattle Farm Worker** (about a fifth of expected)
- Consistent with urban development displacing dairy farming

### 3. **Vineyard Worker** (about a third of expected)
- Liverpool's climate and urban development not conducive to viticulture

*Note: These agricultural shortfalls are appropriate for a suburban LGA and don't indicate economic weaknesses.*

## Economic Character Summary

Liverpool's labourer profile reveals a **suburban industrial hub** with strong food manufacturing and assembly operations. The concentration of factory workers, particularly in food processing and product assembly, suggests Liverpool serves as an important manufacturing center within Greater Sydney's industrial network.
