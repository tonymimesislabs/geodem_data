# Inner West LGA Occupation Analysis: Professionals

## LGA Classification
Inner West is an **urban** LGA in Sydney, NSW, comprising inner-city suburbs like Newtown, Leichhardt, Balmain, and Marrickville. It's known for its creative communities, proximity to Sydney CBD, and diverse cultural scene.

## Overall Assessment
The "Professionals" category appears **significantly over-represented** in Inner West, with numerous occupations showing 2.0x+ representation rates. This reflects the area's appeal to highly educated professionals who value urban amenities and cultural diversity.

## Most Over-Represented Occupations

### 1. **Creative and Media Professionals**
- **Advertising Specialist** (2.9x) - 169 workers
- **Composer** (2.8x) - 10 workers  
- **Actor** (2.6x) - 34 workers
- **Copywriter** (2.5x) - 63 workers

### 2. **Design and Architecture**
- **Architect** (2.4x) - 408 workers
- **Fashion Designer** (2.4x) - 77 workers
- **Interior Designer** (2.1x) - 212 workers

### 3. **Legal Professionals**
- **Barrister** (2.3x) - 179 workers
- **Solicitor** (2.2x) - 1,305 workers

**Economic Indicators**: This concentration suggests Inner West serves as a hub for Sydney's creative industries, design sector, and legal profession. The high numbers of architects and designers align with the area's reputation for urban renewal and creative communities.

## Most Under-Represented Occupations

### 1. **Industrial Sales**
- **Sales Representative (Industrial Products)** (~0.5x) - 109 workers

### 2. **Aged Care Nursing**
- **Registered Nurse (Aged Care)** (~0.5x) - 217 workers

### 3. **Aviation (Non-Commercial)**
- **Helicopter Pilot** (~0.5x) - 7 workers

**Note**: Agricultural and forestry occupations (Forester, Park Ranger, Agricultural Consultant) are appropriately under-represented given the urban setting and shouldn't be considered concerning gaps.

## Summary
Inner West's professional workforce profile reflects a sophisticated urban economy centered on creative industries, professional services, and knowledge work. The concentration of architects, designers, media professionals, and legal practitioners suggests an area that attracts educated professionals seeking urban lifestyle amenities while maintaining proximity to Sydney's CBD and cultural institutions.
