# Fairfield LGA Occupation Analysis: Labourers

## LGA Classification
Fairfield is a **suburban LGA** in western Sydney, NSW. It's a diverse, multicultural area with significant industrial and manufacturing activity, located approximately 30km from Sydney's CBD.

## Overall Assessment
Labourers occupations appear to be **over-represented** in Fairfield, particularly in manufacturing and food processing sectors. The high concentration of factory workers and food processing roles suggests a strong industrial base.

## Most Over-Represented Occupations

### 1. **Baking Factory Worker** (4.3x expected rate)
### 2. **Plastics Factory Worker** (4.3x expected rate) 
### 3. **Product Assembler** (3.3x expected rate, 377 workers)
### 4. **Poultry Process Worker** (3.3x expected rate)
### 5. **Meat Packer** (2.9x expected rate, 176 workers)

**Economic Indicators:** These concentrations strongly indicate Fairfield has a robust manufacturing and food processing industrial base. The high numbers in baking, meat processing, and general manufacturing suggest the area serves as a key industrial hub for Sydney's western suburbs, likely with established food production facilities and light manufacturing plants.

## Most Under-Represented Occupations (Relevant to Suburban Context)

### 1. **Garden Labourer** (about half expected rate)
### 2. **Construction Rigger** (about half expected rate)
### 3. **Paving and Surfacing Labourer** (about half expected rate)
### 4. **Earthmoving Labourer** (about half expected rate)

**Economic Indicators:** The under-representation in construction and landscaping roles suggests Fairfield may have less new development activity compared to other suburban areas, or these services may be sourced from neighboring LGAs.

## Summary
Fairfield's occupation profile reveals a distinctly **industrial suburban character**, with exceptional strength in food processing and light manufacturing. The concentration of factory workers, particularly in food production, positions Fairfield as a key manufacturing hub serving Sydney's broader metropolitan area, reflecting its established industrial infrastructure and multicultural workforce.
