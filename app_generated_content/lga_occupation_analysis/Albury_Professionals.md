# Albury LGA Professional Occupation Analysis

## LGA Classification
Albury is a **regional city** in New South Wales, located on the border with Victoria. It serves as a major regional center for the Murray River region and surrounding rural areas.

## Overall Assessment
Professional occupations in Albury appear to be **slightly under-represented** overall, with most occupations showing "roughly as expected" or "about half the expected rate" compared to national averages. This is typical for regional centers, which generally have fewer professional roles than major metropolitan areas.

## Most Over-Represented Occupations

1. **Forester (1.5x expected rate)** - Reflects Albury's location in a region with significant forestry resources and natural resource management needs
2. **Registered Nurse (Aged Care) (slightly above expected)** - Indicates a strong healthcare sector serving the regional population, particularly aged care services
3. **Conservation Officer (roughly as expected, but notable presence)** - Aligns with the region's environmental management needs along the Murray River system

## Most Under-Represented Occupations

1. **Software Engineer (about a third of expected)** - Limited tech industry presence typical of regional areas
2. **ICT Business Analyst (about a third of expected)** - Reflects smaller corporate/tech sector compared to major cities
3. **Architect (about a third of expected)** - Lower construction/development activity than metropolitan areas
4. **Management Consultant (about half expected)** - Fewer large corporations requiring specialized consulting services
5. **Marketing Specialist (about half expected)** - Smaller commercial sector with less specialized marketing roles

## Summary
Albury's professional occupation profile reflects its role as a regional service center with strengths in healthcare, education, and natural resource management. The under-representation of ICT and business consulting roles is typical for regional Australia, while the presence of forestry and conservation professionals highlights its connection to the surrounding natural environment and primary industries.
