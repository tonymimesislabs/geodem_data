# Willoughby LGA Occupation Analysis: Managers

## LGA Classification
Willoughby is a **suburban LGA** located on Sydney's North Shore, characterized by affluent residential areas with good access to Sydney's CBD and major business districts.

## Overall Assessment
Manager occupations are **moderately over-represented** in Willoughby, with several key management roles showing significantly higher concentrations than the national average, reflecting the area's affluent, professional character.

## Most Over-Represented Occupations

### Highly Over-Represented (2.0x+ expected rate):
- **Advertising Manager** (2.2x) - Indicates proximity to Sydney's creative and marketing industries
- **Importer/Exporter** (2.2x) - Reflects Sydney's role as a major trade hub and international gateway
- **Chief Information Officer** (2.0x) - Shows concentration of senior tech leadership roles

### Notably Over-Represented (1.5x expected rate):
- **Chief Executive/Managing Director** (474 workers, 1.5x) - Large absolute numbers suggest many senior executives choose to live in this affluent area
- **ICT Project Manager** (290 workers, 1.5x) - Strong technology sector representation
- **Finance Manager** (346 workers, 1.5x) - Proximity to Sydney's financial district

## Most Under-Represented Occupations (Excluding Rural/Agricultural)

- **Senior Non-commissioned Defence Force Member** (0.5x expected) - Limited military presence typical for suburban areas
- **Licensed Club Manager** (0.5x expected) - Fewer traditional clubs in this affluent suburban setting
- **Transport Company Manager** (0.5x expected) - Limited industrial/logistics operations in residential area
- **Post Office Manager** (0.5x expected) - Reflects broader trend of postal service consolidation
- **Equipment Hire Manager** (0.5x expected) - Limited industrial services in suburban residential area

## Summary
Willoughby's management occupation profile reflects a **highly educated, affluent suburban community** with strong connections to Sydney's CBD professional services, technology, and international business sectors. The concentration of senior executives, finance managers, and ICT professionals indicates this area serves as a preferred residential location for Sydney's business elite, while the under-representation of industrial and service management roles aligns with its primarily residential, white-collar character.
